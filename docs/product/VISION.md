# Product Vision — The Gift of Memory

## Core Principle

My Parents' Story is not an app people download. It's a gift people give.

That single sentence influences onboarding, sharing, branding, and growth.

## The Emotional Question

Most apps ask: "What do you want to do?"

This app should ask: **"Whose story would you never want to lose?"**

That question immediately changes the mindset from productivity to legacy.

## The First Memory Experience

The first 5 minutes determine whether someone feels they are using software or creating something meaningful.

### Flow:

```
Welcome
  "Every family has stories that deserve to be remembered."

  ↓

Choose Someone
  "Who would you like to preserve today?"
  
  Mother
  Father
  Grandmother
  Grandfather
  Someone special

  ↓

Before the first question
  "There are no right or wrong answers.
   Just tell your story, one memory at a time."

  Then the first question appears.
```

## Sharing as Giving

Don't encourage users to "Share the app."

Instead, let them share moments.

### Examples:
- A beautifully designed quote card from a parent's story
- A short audio clip with permission
- A memory card with a childhood photo
- A page from the generated memoir

The shared content should naturally make someone ask: **"What app is this?"**

The story becomes the marketing.

## The Gift Loop

Growth loop designed around families:

```
Daughter records her mother
  → She generates a beautiful memoir
    → She gifts it to her siblings
      → They decide to record their father
        → The extended family begins preserving stories
```

Growth comes from love and remembrance, not invitations or referral codes.

## The "One in a Lifetime" Moment

Every user should eventually reach a screen:

> "Today you've preserved something that can never be recreated once it's gone.
> Thank you for protecting your family's story."

That's the emotional payoff — not a badge or a streak.

## Trust Before Growth

Communicate clearly:
- Your recordings belong to you
- Nothing is shared without your permission
- Your memories stay under your control
- You can export everything at any time

When people trust the app with their family's stories, they're far more likely to recommend it.

## Mission

> Every family deserves to preserve the voice, stories, wisdom, and legacy of the people they love — before those memories are lost.

That is a mission people can support, not just a feature they can use.

## Success Metrics (Outcomes, Not Downloads)

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| Families who completed their first memory | Proof of value |
| Parents who recorded multiple chapters | Depth of engagement |
| Memoirs generated | Tangible output |
| Memories safely preserved | Trust earned |
| Families who invited another family | Organic growth from meaning |

If those numbers grow, downloads follow. The app becomes something people recommend because it helped them preserve something irreplaceable.
