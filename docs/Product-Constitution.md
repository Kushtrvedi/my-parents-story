# Product Constitution v1.0

> **Every family deserves to preserve the voice, wisdom, memories, and legacy of their parents — for free, forever.**

This document governs every product decision. No feature ships unless it aligns with these principles.

---

## The Five Non-Negotiable Pillars

### 1. Free Forever

The core experience will always remain free.

Includes:
- Unlimited parents
- Unlimited memories
- Unlimited voice recordings
- Unlimited chapters
- Unlimited PDFs
- Unlimited local storage
- Unlimited languages supported by the device

No ads. No subscriptions. No paywall around memories.

### 2. Privacy First

Parents' memories belong only to the family.

Default behavior:
- Store everything locally
- No mandatory account
- No cloud processing by default
- No selling or analyzing personal data
- Cloud backup is optional and user-controlled

### 3. Voice First

The app is designed for speaking, not typing.

Priority order:
1. Listen
2. Speak
3. Read
4. Type

Typing is a fallback.

### 4. Elderly First

Every design decision must answer:

> "Could my 75-year-old parent use this without help?"

Design standards:
- Large touch targets (minimum 60px)
- Large fonts (minimum 22px)
- High contrast
- Minimal text
- One primary action per screen
- No hidden gestures
- No technical terminology

### 5. Legacy Over Productivity

Never use:
- Tasks, goals, streaks, scores, XP, levels, achievement badges

Instead use:
- Chapters, memories, stories, legacy, family archive

## Product Principles

The app should never feel like:
- ChatGPT
- A journal
- A note-taking app
- A voice recorder
- A survey
- A chatbot

It should feel like:

> A patient grandchild sitting beside a parent, listening carefully and preserving every precious story.

## The Core Principle

My Parents' Story is not an app people download. It's a gift people give.

Every design decision should influence onboarding, sharing, branding, and growth to reflect this core principle. Rather than asking "What do you want to do?", the app asks "Whose story would you never want to lose?".

## Growth & The Gift Loop

Growth comes from love and remembrance, not invitations or referral codes.
Sharing should never feel like marketing. It should feel like giving.

The Gift Loop:
- A daughter records her mother.
- She generates a beautiful memoir.
- She gifts it to her siblings.
- They decide to record their father.
- The extended family begins preserving stories.

The shared content (a beautifully designed quote card, a short audio clip, or a memoir page) should naturally make someone ask "What app is this?"—the story becomes the marketing.

## Measuring Success

Do not measure success by downloads. Measure it by outcomes:
- Families who completed their first memory.
- Parents who recorded multiple chapters.
- Memoirs generated.
- Memories safely preserved.
- Families who invited another family because the experience mattered to them.

## Long-Term Vision

You are not building another app.

You are building a **Family Legacy Operating System**.

Today it captures memories. Tomorrow it preserves family history. In the future, it becomes a trusted archive where generations can hear voices, read stories, and understand the people who came before them.
