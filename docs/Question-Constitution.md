# Question Constitution v1.0

> **Every question is an act of preservation. Each one unlocks a memory that would otherwise be lost forever.**

This document governs how every question in the system is designed, ordered, and delivered.

---

## Purpose

The question system is the backbone of every autobiography this application generates.

These are not survey questions.

These are not interview prompts.

They are gentle invitations to remember — designed to help a parent tell the story of their life to someone they love.

---

## The Three Rules

### Rule 1: Every question must create a memory

A question that gets a yes/no answer is a failed question.

A question that creates a paragraph is a successful question.

The goal is always story-rich responses that capture detail, emotion, and meaning.

### Rule 2: Every question must be speakable

The parent should be able to hear the question read aloud and immediately begin answering.

No question should take more than fifteen seconds to read aloud.

No question should require the parent to think about what is being asked.

### Rule 3: Every question must be kind

The parent is sharing their life with us.

We must never judge, never probe for pain, never rush, and never make them feel uncomfortable.

If a question could cause distress, it must be phrased with warmth and an easy exit.

---

## Emotional Tones

Every question carries an emotional tone that guides how it should be delivered:

| Tone | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Warm** | Creates comfort and safety | "Tell me about your mother. What was she like?" |
| **Joyful** | Celebrates happy memories | "What was the happiest day of your childhood?" |
| **Curious** | Explores without pressure | "I wonder what your first day of school was like?" |
| **Reflective** | Invites deep thought | "Looking back, what matters most about those years?" |
| **Proud** | Honors achievements | "What accomplishment are you most proud of?" |
| **Nostalgic** | Evokes sensory memories | "What did your home smell like when you were little?" |
| **Hopeful** | Looks forward with optimism | "What do you hope your grandchildren will carry forward?" |
| **Legacy** | Connects to meaning | "What wisdom would you pass on to someone just starting life?" |

## Question Design Rules

### Always

- Use simple, clear language
- Address the parent directly ("you", "your")
- Use past tense for memories, present tense for ongoing feelings
- Encourage storytelling and specific details
- End every chapter on a positive or hopeful note
- Begin every chapter with easy, warm questions
- Gradually deepen emotional depth within each chapter
- Support translation into any language without losing meaning

### Never

- Ask compound questions (two questions in one)
- Use jargon, idioms, or culturally specific references
- Ask leading questions that suggest an answer
- Ask about politics, religion, or controversial topics unless volunteered
- Probe for trauma or painful memories
- Ask rapid-fire questions in succession
- Use phrases like "Don't you think..." or "Wouldn't you agree..."
- Ask about financial details unless volunteered
- Ask medical or health diagnosis questions

### Avoid

- Questions that can be answered with yes or no
- Questions that assume a specific life experience
- Questions that require specialized knowledge
- Questions that could embarrass or shame
- Questions about sensitive legal matters

## Question Flow

Each question includes:

### Core Question
The primary question asked of the parent.

### Follow-up Questions (5-10)
Conditional questions that depend on the parent's answer. These are suggested to the interviewer or used by the AI to deepen the conversation.

### Purpose
Why this question is asked. What memory it is designed to unlock.

### Expected Memory Type
What kind of memory this question typically produces:
- **Sensory**: Smells, sounds, sights, textures
- **Emotional**: Feelings, moods, atmosphere
- **Narrative**: Stories with beginning, middle, end
- **Factual**: Dates, names, places, events
- **Reflective**: Lessons, meaning, wisdom

### Emotional Tone
The feeling this question should evoke.

### Estimated Duration
How long a typical answer takes: Short (<30s), Medium (30-90s), Long (90s+).

### Search Tags
Keywords for searching and organizing memories.

### People
Types of people this question typically involves.

### Places
Types of places this question typically involves.

### Suitable Age Range
The age range of the parent for whom this question is most appropriate.

### Difficulty
How emotionally challenging this question might be: Easy, Moderate, Sensitive.

### Priority
How important this question is to the overall memoir: Core, Important, Optional.

## Localization

Every question must translate naturally into:
- English
- Hindi
- Gujarati
- Spanish

Rules:
- No idioms that only work in one language
- No cultural references specific to one country
- Questions should feel natural in every language
- Meaning must be preserved, not just words

---

## Follow-up System

Follow-up questions are the heart of the guided conversation.

When a parent answers a core question, the system suggests follow-ups that:
1. Deepen the specific memory
2. Explore emotional aspects
3. Connect to related memories
4. Uncover details that make the story vivid

### Follow-up Rules

- Always relate to the parent's actual answer
- Never feel like a separate question
- Build naturally on what was just said
- Help the parent add color, detail, and emotion
- Can be offered gently: "That's beautiful. Can you tell me more about...?"

## Quality Assurance

Every question is validated against these criteria:

1. **Readability**: Under 15 seconds to read aloud
2. **Clarity**: Understandable by a 75-year-old
3. **Warmth**: Feels like it comes from a caring grandchild
4. **Story Potential**: Encourages a narrative response
5. **Safety**: No risk of causing distress
6. **Translatability**: Works in all supported languages
7. **Uniqueness**: No duplicate or near-duplicate questions
8. **Ordering**: Appropriate for its position in the chapter
