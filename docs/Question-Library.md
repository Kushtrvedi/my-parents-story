# Question Library — My Parents Story

> Complete reference for the 300-question interview system powering the family legacy experience.

---

## Overview

The **My Parents Story** question library is the backbone of the family legacy recording system. It guides interviewers (and AI interviewers) through a structured, emotionally intelligent conversation designed to preserve a parent's life story in their own words.

### Structure

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Total questions | 300 |
| Chapters | 20 |
| Questions per chapter | 15 |
| Estimated total session time | 15–25 hours (across multiple sessions) |
| Average question duration | 3–8 minutes |

### Question Metadata

Each question includes:

- **ID** — Unique identifier (format: C{chapter}Q{question}, e.g., C01Q01)
- **Question text** — The exact prompt spoken to the parent
- **Purpose** — Why this question matters and what it aims to uncover
- **Emotional tone** — The emotional register of the question (neutral, warm, reflective, gentle, celebratory, poignant, hopeful, probing, lighthearted)
- **Expected memory type** — The cognitive category of memory expected (episodic, semantic, procedural, emotional, sensory, narrative, autobiographical)
- **Estimated duration** — How long the response typically takes (quick, short, medium, long, extended)
- **Difficulty** — Emotional and cognitive difficulty (easy, moderate, challenging, deep)
- **Follow-up questions** — 5–10 follow-up prompts to deepen the response

### Emotional Journey

The chapters are sequenced to follow an emotional arc:

1. **Chapters 1–4** — Warm-up and foundational (low emotional intensity)
2. **Chapters 5–8** — Personal growth and identity (building trust)
3. **Chapters 9–12** — Relationships and family (emotional core)
4. **Chapters 13–16** — Meaning-making and resilience (deeper reflection)
5. **Chapters 17–20** — Legacy and reflection (culmination)

### Usage Guidelines

- **Pace:** Do not rush. Allow silence after deep questions.
- **Order:** Chapters are designed to flow sequentially, but can be reordered based on the parent's comfort.
- **Selection:** Not all 300 questions need to be asked. Choose 100–150 for a core session; use the rest for deep-dive sessions.
- **Recording:** Always record audio/video. The question library is a guide, not a script.
- **Sensitivity:** Monitor emotional state. Skip or pause on questions that cause distress.
- **Follow-ups:** Follow-up questions are suggestions. Adapt based on the parent's responses.

## Chapter 1: Roots and Family Origins

*Exploring the parent's heritage, ancestry, and the family story that came before them.*

---

### C01Q01

**Question:** "Can you tell me the story of your family name — where does it come from, and what do you know about its history?"

**Purpose:** Establishes a sense of lineage and identity. Surname history often carries cultural, occupational, or geographic significance that grounds the entire interview.

**Emotional tone:** Warm, curious

**Expected memory type:** Semantic (family lore and facts)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Has the name changed over generations — spelling, pronunciation, or anglicization?
2. Are there any stories about why your ancestors settled where they did?
3. Do you know what the name means in your ancestral language?
4. Have you ever looked up the name in any records or databases?
5. Did anyone in your family ever change the name, and why?
6. Is there a branch of the family that spells it differently?
7. How did it feel to carry this name growing up?
8. Did your grandparents ever talk about the name's history?

---

### C01Q02

**Question:** "What do you know about your grandparents — the ones you may or may not have met?"

**Purpose:** Reaches back one or two generations to capture family knowledge that may be lost if not recorded now.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective, nostalgic

**Expected memory type:** Narrative (stories passed down)

**Estimated duration:** Long (5–8 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Which grandparents do you remember most clearly, and why?
2. What were their personalities like, based on what you were told?
3. Did any of them have unusual occupations or life paths?
4. What was the relationship like between your parents and their parents?
5. Did you inherit anything physical from any of your grandparents?
6. Were there any family feuds or estrangements you know about?
7. What language did your grandparents speak at home?
8. Is there a family legend about any of your grandparents that you loved hearing?
9. What happened to your grandparents during major historical events?
10. If you could ask one grandparent one question, what would it be?

---

### C01Q03

**Question:** "Where were your parents born, and how did their families end up there?"

**Purpose:** Maps the geographic origins of both parental lines and the migration stories behind them.

**Emotional tone:** Neutral, narrative

**Expected memory type:** Narrative (family migration story)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Did your parents' families move for economic, political, or personal reasons?
2. How far apart were your parents' birthplaces from each other?
3. What was the town or village like where your mother was born?
4. What was the town or village like where your father was born?
5. Did any family members make significant migrations — across borders, across oceans?
6. Were there any family members who stayed behind when others moved?
7. Did your parents ever talk about wanting to return to their birthplace?
8. Are there relatives still living in those original places?

### C01Q04

**Question:** "Tell me about your ethnic or cultural background — what groups or traditions do you come from?"

**Purpose:** Explores the cultural tapestry of the parent's heritage, including any mixed or complex backgrounds.

**Emotional tone:** Warm, respectful

**Expected memory type:** Semantic (cultural knowledge)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Were there multiple cultures represented in your family, and how did they blend?
2. Was your cultural background something your family celebrated or kept quiet?
3. Did you ever feel caught between different cultural identities?
4. Were there cultural traditions your family practiced that you didn't understand as a child?
5. How did your cultural background affect the way others treated you growing up?
6. Did your parents speak different languages at home?
7. Were there foods, clothes, or customs that marked your family as different?
8. How do you feel about your cultural heritage now versus when you were young?

### C01Q05

**Question:** "Do you know anything about your family's socioeconomic background — were they farmers, merchants, professionals, laborers?"

**Purpose:** Uncovers the class and economic history of the family, which shapes opportunity and worldview.

**Emotional tone:** Neutral, matter-of-fact

**Expected memory type:** Semantic (family facts)

**Estimated duration:** Short (2–4 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Did your family's economic status change across generations?
2. Was there a time when the family was notably wealthy or notably poor?
3. Did anyone in your family break out of the expected economic class?
4. How did your parents talk about money when you were growing up?
5. Were there objects or heirlooms that symbolized the family's economic history?
6. Did the family own land or property at any point?
7. How did economic circumstances shape the family's choices about where to live?

### C01Q06

**Question:** "Were there any notable or famous people in your family tree, or anyone with an extraordinary story?"

8. Are there any documents, photos, or artifacts connected to this person?

### C01Q07

**Question:** "What family stories were told over and over when you were growing up — the ones everyone seemed to know by heart?"

**Emotional tone:** Warm, nostalgic

5. Did you ever hear a version of a story that shocked you because it was so different?

7. Did any of these stories turn out to be not entirely true?
8. Were there stories that were considered too sensitive to tell in certain company?

### C01Q08

**Question:** "What was the relationship like between your parents' families before your parents married?"

**Purpose:** Explores the pre-existing family dynamics and any alliances, rivalries, or connections between the two sides.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective

**Expected memory type:** Narrative (family dynamics)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**

2. Were the two families similar or very different from each other?
3. Did anyone on either side oppose the marriage?
4. What did your grandparents think of each other?
5. Were there cultural, religious, or class differences between the families?
6. How did the two families get along at gatherings after the marriage?
7. Did you feel closer to one side of the family than the other?
8. Were there any rivalries or competition between the two families?

5. Did anyone in the family resist the move?

7. Did the family maintain ties with the place they left?
8. How many generations did it take for the family to feel settled?

### C01Q10

**Question:** "Were there any family secrets — things that were kept hidden or not spoken about openly?"

**Difficulty:** Moderate

**Follow-up questions:**

4. Do you think the secret was justified in being kept?
5. How did it feel to learn something surprising about your family?
6. Are there things you think your parents never told you?
7. Did the secret affect how the family interacted with outsiders?
8. Would you want your own children to know about this secret?

### C01Q11

**Question:** "What role did your family's religion or spiritual tradition play in your upbringing?"

**Purpose:** Explores the spiritual dimension of the family's identity and how it was transmitted across generations.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective, respectful

8. Did you pass on the same religious traditions to your own children?

### C01Q13

**Question:** "Do you know anything about your ancestors' occupations — what did they do for a living across generations?"

**Purpose:** Uncovers the occupational heritage of the family, which often reveals class, migration, and survival patterns.

### C01Q14

**Question:** "Was there a family home or place — a house, a village, a piece of land — that holds special significance?"

**Purpose:** Identifies the physical places that anchor the family's sense of belonging and memory.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective, nostalgic

**Expected memory type:** Episodic (place-based memory)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**

2. Who lived there, and what was life like in that home?

5. What did this place smell or sound like?
6. Were there specific rooms or spots that held significance?
7. Did anything important happen at this place — a birth, a death, a celebration?

9. Do you have any photographs of this place?
10. If you could visit this place one more time, what would you do there?

### C01Q15

**Question:** "If you could go back in time and meet one of your ancestors, who would it be and what would you ask them?"

**Purpose:** A reflective closing question that invites the parent to articulate what matters most about their family history.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective, imaginative

**Expected memory type:** Autobiographical (reflective)

**Estimated duration:** Short (2–4 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Why this ancestor in particular?
2. What era would you want to visit them in?
3. Would you tell them about your life, or just listen?
4. What do you think they would be most surprised about regarding your life?
5. Is there something you wish you had asked your own parents or grandparents?
6. What do you think you have in common with this ancestor?
7. Is there a question that no one in the family can answer about your history?
8. Do you think this ancestor would be proud of how the family turned out?

## Chapter 2: Birth and Early Childhood

*The first stories — pregnancy, birth, and the earliest memories of life.*

---

### C02Q01

**Question:** "Can you tell me about the circumstances of your birth — where, when, and what your parents have told you about that time?"

**Purpose:** Opens with the most foundational moment of the parent's life story, often rich with family lore and emotion.

**Emotional tone:** Warm, celebratory

**Expected memory type:** Narrative (told story, not remembered)

**Estimated duration:** Medium (3–5 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Follow-up questions:**
1. Was your birth expected or a surprise?
2. Was it a difficult birth, or did things go smoothly?
3. Were you born at home or in a hospital?
4. Who was present at your birth?
5. Were you named immediately, or did it take time to choose a name?
6. Was there a story about how your name was chosen?
7. Were you the firstborn, and did that matter?
8. What was your mother's health like after your birth?
9. Did anyone travel to see you after you were born?
10. How did your parents describe the day you were born?

---

### C02Q02

**Question:** "What is the earliest memory you have — the very first thing you can recall from your childhood?"

**Purpose:** Invites the parent to access their oldest autobiographical memory, which often sets the emotional tone for their early years.

**Emotional tone:** Reflective, gentle

**Expected memory type:** Episodic (personal memory)

**Estimated duration:** Short (2–4 minutes)

**Difficulty:** Moderate

**Follow-up questions:**
1. How old do you think you were in this memory?
2. Can you describe the sensory details — what you saw, heard, smelled?
3. Who else was in the memory?
4. Was this a happy memory, or something more difficult?
5. How confident are you that this memory is accurate?
6. Are there other very early memories that come to mind?
7. Do you think this memory has been shaped by stories you were told?
8. What is the feeling that stays with you from this memory?

---

### C02Q03

## Chapter 3: Home and Neighbourhood

*The world beyond the front door — the places, people, and experiences that shaped early life.*

---

### C03Q01

**Question:** "Tell me about the neighbourhood you grew up in — what was it like to live there?"
