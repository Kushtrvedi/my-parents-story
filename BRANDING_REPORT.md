## Executive Summary

After researching all 10 candidate names against Play Store competition, trademark risk, domain availability, memorability, international readability, emotional resonance, SEO/discoverability, and long-term scalability, the recommendation is:

**RECOMMENDATION: Keep "My Parents' Story"**

The current name is the strongest option available. It has zero direct Play Store competition, high emotional clarity, excellent international readability, and is immediately distinguishable from every competitor in the space. No alternative offers a clear advantage that justifies the cost of renaming.

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. My Parents' Story (Score: 57) — RECOMMENDED

**Play Store**: No exact match found. Closest competitors are "Memoir," "StoryWorth," "Remento," "Memory Murals," "HereAfter AI" — all different names in the same space.

**Trademark**: Descriptive, but no direct conflicts found. Could potentially register as a design mark with logo.

**Domain**: myparentsstory.com exists but is an unrelated AI photo/video service (different market). Could pursue myparentsstory.app or alternative TLD.

**Strengths**:
- Immediately tells you what the app does
- Emotionally direct — "My Parents'" creates personal ownership
- Works as a conversation starter: "Have you tried My Parents' Story?"
- Perfectly aligned with the Constitution's "parents" focus
- Translates cleanly across all 12 supported languages

**Weaknesses**:
- Descriptive names are harder to trademark
- Longer than single-word alternatives
- "Story" is slightly generic in this space

**Why it wins**: Zero competition + maximum clarity + emotional precision. The name IS the value proposition.

---

### 2. Voice of Home (Score: 52) — STRONG ALTERNATIVE

**Play Store**: No exact match found. "Voice Access" and "Voice Dream Reader" exist but are unrelated.

**Trademark**: More distinctive than "My Parents' Story" — could potentially register as a word mark.

**Domain**: voiceofhome.com appears available (no active site found).

**Strengths**:
- Most poetic and distinctive name in the set
- "Voice" = what the app captures
- "Home" = where family lives
- Low SEO competition
- High differentiation

**Weaknesses**:
- "Voice" may confuse users expecting a voice assistant
- Less immediately obvious what the app does
- "Home" doesn't directly reference family/parents
- Longer name (4 words)

**Why it loses**: Strong branding, but less immediately clear than "My Parents' Story." Users seeing "Voice of Home" in search results may not immediately understand it's for preserving parent stories.

### 3. The Story We Keep (Score: 50) — POETIC ALTERNATIVE

**Play Store**: No exact match found.

**Trademark**: The full phrase "The Story We Keep" could potentially register as a phrase mark.

**Domain**: thestorywekeep.com appears available.

**Strengths**:
- Most poetic name in the set
- "We Keep" implies active, intentional preservation
- Low competition
- High differentiation

**Weaknesses**:
- Less immediately obvious than "My Parents' Story"
- "We" is vague — who is "we"?
- Doesn't directly reference parents
- 4 words, longer than ideal

**Why it loses**: Beautiful name, but sacrifices the emotional directness of "My Parents' Story."

### 4. Roots (Score: 36) — DISQUALIFIED

**Play Store**: "Roots: Screen Time Control" (popular app, 4.8 stars), "Family Root" (genealogy app). "Roots" is also associated with RootsTech (major genealogy conference).

**Trademark**: "Roots" is generic for genealogy/family history. Nearly impossible to protect.

**Domain**: roots.com taken, getroots.app in use by screen time app.

**Why it loses**: Too much existing competition. "Roots" = genealogy search in user minds, not story preservation.

**Why it loses**: Domain taken by active competitor. Name too similar to existing service.

## Recommendation

**Keep "My Parents' Story."**

### Rationale:

1. **Zero direct competition** — No app on Google Play uses this exact name
2. **Maximum clarity** — Users immediately understand what the app does
3. **Emotional precision** — "My Parents'" creates personal ownership and urgency
4. **Mission alignment** — Perfectly matches the Constitution's focus on "parents"
5. **International readability** — Translates cleanly across all 12 languages
6. **Conversation-ready** — "Have you tried My Parents' Story?" works naturally
7. **No renaming cost** — Zero code changes, zero migration, zero confusion
8. **Scalability** — Name supports future features (grandparents, other family members)

### Trade-offs to accept:
- Descriptive names are harder to trademark (but no direct conflicts exist)
- 4 words is longer than ideal (but "Memoir" and "Legacy" are taken)
- "Story" is slightly generic (but combined with "My Parents'" it's distinctive)
